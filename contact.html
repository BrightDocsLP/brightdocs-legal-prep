Frequently Asked Questions

What is a legal document preparer?

A legal document preparer is a trained professional authorized to prepare legal documents at the direction of a client, but they cannot offer legal advice or representation.

What types of legal documents can BrightDocs prepare?

BrightDocs prepares a variety of documents, including estate planning documents, probate documents, family law documents, property deeds, and business formation documents.

  • Estate planning: wills, trusts, powers of attorney, and living wills
  • Probate documents: small estate affidavits and informal probate documents
  • Family law documents: divorce, legal separation, consent decrees, and QDROs
  • Property documents: deeds, owner carryback agreements, promissory notes, and deed of trust documents
  • Business documents: LLC formation and S Corp election documents

If you are unsure whether BrightDocs can prepare your specific document, contact us and we will guide you.

Do I need an attorney to use BrightDocs?

No. BrightDocs provides legal document preparation services, not legal advice. You do not need an attorney to have your documents prepared with us. If your situation requires legal advice or representation, we recommend speaking with a licensed attorney.

How does the consultation fee work?

A consultation fee applies to your first appointment. If you choose to have BrightDocs prepare your documents, the consultation fee is credited toward your total cost. This ensures you only pay once for the support you need.

Can you meet me at my home?

Yes. You can meet at our office or request a home visit. Let us know when scheduling your consultation, and we will make arrangements based on availability.

How long does it take to prepare documents?

The timeline depends on the type and complexity of your documents. Most standard documents can be prepared within a few days after your consultation. BrightDocs will provide an estimated timeline during your appointment.

Do you handle court filings or legal representation?

BrightDocs prepares legal documents but does not provide legal representation in court, act as a title company, or manage escrow or funds transfers. Clients are responsible for filing documents with the appropriate court or office unless other arrangements are discussed.

Are there additional fees beyond the listed prices?

All standard pricing is discussed before work begins. Additional fees may apply if your situation is more complex, requires multiple properties, involves unusual family circumstances, or needs extra documents. You will receive a complete quote before moving forward.

How can I contact BrightDocs if I have more questions?

You can use the contact form below, call, or email us. You can also schedule a consultation to discuss your specific needs in more detail.

How long does a divorce or legal separation take?

For uncontested cases where both parties are in agreement and no changes are made, the process usually takes about two and a half to three months after filing, depending on the court's schedule. Contested cases, where disagreements need to be resolved, typically take longer.

What is a QDRO?

A Qualified Domestic Relations Order, or QDRO, is a legal order used to divide certain types of retirement plans, such as 401(k), 403(b), and pension benefits, between parties in a divorce or separation. QDROs are required under federal law for most employer-sponsored retirement plans. Government retirement plans are usually exempt, but many still require a court order to divide benefits.

IRAs typically do not require a QDRO for division.
