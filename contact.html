Frequently Asked Questions

What is a legal document preparer?

A legal document preparer is a trained professional authorized to prepare legal documents at the direction of a client, but who cannot offer legal advice or representation.

What types of legal documents can Brightdocs prepare?

We prepare a wide range of documents, including:

  • Estate planning: wills, trusts, powers of attorney, living wills
  • Probate documents: small estate affidavits, informal probate
  • Family law: divorce, legal separations, consent decrees, QDROs
  • Property: deeds, carryback agreements, promissory notes
  • Business: LLC formation, S-Corp election

f you're unsure whether we can prepare your specific document, contact us and we'll guide you.

Do I need an attorney to use BrightDocs?

No. Brightdocs provides legal document preparation services, not legal advice. You don't need an attorney to have your documents prepared with us. If you need legal advice, we recommend consulting a licensed attorney.

How does the consultation fee work?

A consultation fee applies to your first appointment. If you choose to have BrightDocs prepare your documents, the fee is credited toward your total cost. This ensures you only pay once for your services.

Can you meet me at my home?

Yes! We can meet you at your home, through Zoom, or a public space like Starbucks or a public library. When scheduling the appointment, choose the best option for you. If you choose your home, don't forget to verify that your address is correct.

How long does it take to prepare documents?

The timeline varies depending on the type and complexity of your documents. Most standard documents can be prepared within a few days after your consultation. We will provide an estimated timeline during your appointment.

Do you handle court filings or legal representation?

No. BrightDocs prepares legal documents but does not provide legal representation in court, act as a title company, or manage escrow or funds transfers. Clients are responsible for filing documents with the appropriate court or office.

Are there additional fees beyond the listed prices?

All standard pricing is disclosed during your consultation. Additional fees may apply if your situation is more complex, such as multiple properties, unusual family circumstances, or additional documents required. We always provide a complete, upfront quote.

How can I contact BrightDocs if I have more questions?

You can reach us through the contact form, by phone, or by email. You can also schedule a consultation to discuss your needs in detail.

How long does a divorce or legal separation take?

For uncontested cases where both parties are in agreement, and no changes are made, the process usually takes about 2 and a half to 3 months upon filing, depending on the court's schedule. Contested cases, where disagreements need to be resolved, typically take longer.

What is a QDRO?

A Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO) is a legal order used to divide certain types of retirement plans, such as 401(k)s, 403(b)s, and pensions, between parties in a divorce or separation. QDROs are required under federal law (ERISA) for most employer-sponsored retirement plans. Government retirement plans are usually exempt, but many still require a court order to divide benefits.

Note that IRAs typically do not require a QDRO for division.
