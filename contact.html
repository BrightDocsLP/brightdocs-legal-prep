Frequently Asked Questions

What is an Arizona Certified Legal Document Preparer (AZCLDP)?

An individual or business who is certified by the Arizona Supreme Court to prepare legal documents and provide general legal factual information, without the supervision of an attorney, for individuals or businesses who represent themselves in any legal matter.
BrightDocs Legal Prep, LLC is a Certified Legal Document Preparer.

What is the difference between an Arizona Certified Legal Document Preparer (AZCLDP) and a paralegal?

A paralegal works under the supervision of a licensed attorney. A certified legal document preparer does not require supervision by a licensed attorney. Both must avoid the unauthorized practice of law.

Can an Arizona Certified Legal Document Preparer (AZCLDP) give me legal advice or represent me in Court?

Great question! The simple answer is “no.” An AZCLDP cannot present themselves as attorneys, give you legal advice or represent you in Court. For individuals who simply need an understanding of legal processes, do not need legal counsel, and are not represented by counsel, BrightDocs Legal Prep, LLC is a great fit to meet legal needs, such as:

  • Prepare legal documents,
  • Provide legal information and options,
  • File documents with the county superior court in Arizona,
  • Record documents with the county recorder's office in Arizona, and
  • Submit documents with the Arizona Corporation Commission.

Please note: For document filing, recording, and submission services, documents must be prepared by BrightDocs Legal Prep, LLC.

What types of legal documents can BrightDocs prepare?

We prepare a wide range of documents, including:

  • Estate planning: wills, trusts, powers of attorney, living wills
  • Probate documents: small estate affidavits, informal probate
  • Family law: divorce, legal separations, consent decrees, QDROs
  • Property: deeds, carryback agreements, promissory notes
  • Business: LLC formation

If you're unsure whether we can prepare your specific document, contact us and we'll guide you.

How long does it take to prepare documents?

The timeline varies depending on the type and complexity of your documents, as well as calendaring. Most standard documents can be prepared within a few days to a couple weeks after your consultation. We will provide an estimated timeline during your appointment.

You can reach us through the contact form, by phone, or by email at contact@brightdocslegalprep.com

How does the consultation fee work?

A consultation fee applies to your first appointment. If you choose to have BrightDocs prepare your documents, the fee will be credited toward any cost for document preparation. This ensures you only pay once for your services.

Can you meet me at my home?

Yes! We are a mobile service company. We can meet at your home, via Zoom, or other convenient location. Just let us know when scheduling your consultation, and we'll make the arrangements. All signing appointments are in person.
